I am a research fellow at Microsoft Research India, where I work with Dr. Indrani Medhi Thies and Dr. Mohit Jain in the Technology and Empowerment Group (TEM). I am interested in working on research problems around information, communication, and technologies for development, especially in the Global South.

My research interests lie in understanding the emergent socio-technical practices of marginalized populations to design more inclusive and culturally-appropriate technologies that can meaningfully impact their lives. More specifically, I aspire to use ethnographic research methods to investigate, engage with, and harness the existing social, cultural, and digital infrastructures embedded within communities during technology design. I am most excited to learn about and contribute to issues concerning healthcare and well-being, gender, and the future of work. As a woman born and brought up in the Global South, my interests have been strongly shaped by not just my own lived experiences, but also reflecting on the silent and (not so) silent interactions all these years!

Before joining Microsoft Research, I graduated with a bachelor's degree (honors) in computer science engineering from IIIT Delhi. At IIIT Delhi, I was fortunate to work and be mentored by Dr. Grace Eden and Dr. Pushpendra Singh. For my undergraduate thesis, I worked on understanding how people interpret air quality information, and how it informs their understanding of risk.

I am applying to PhD programs in HCI and Information Sciences for Fall 2023!
