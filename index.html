I am a research fellow at Microsoft Research India, where I work with Dr. Indrani Medhi Thies and Dr. Mohit Jain in the Technology and Empowerment Group (TEM). I am interested in working on research problems around information, communication, and technologies for development, especially in the Global South.

More specifically, my research interests lie in critically reflecting and designing culturally appropriate technologies for marginalized communities, especially in the Global South, using ethnographic research methods. I am deeply passionate about working on issues around healthcare and well-being, environment, social justice, religion, and caste. I wish to engage with communities whose voices often go unheard, and hope to narrate their stories through my work. As a woman born and brought up in the Global South, my interests have been strongly shaped by not just my own lived experiences, but also reflecting the silent and un-silent interactions I've had with numerous from disadvantaged groups all these years!.

Before joining Microsoft Research, I graduated with a bachelor's degree (honors) in computer science engineering from IIIT Delhi. At IIIT Delhi, I was fortunate to work and be mentored by Dr. Grace Eden and Dr. Pushpendra Singh. For my undergraduate thesis, I worked on understanding how people interpret air quality information, and how it informs their understanding of risk.
